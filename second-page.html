    • What is Treaty 1?

    Treaty 1 was a treaty made on August 3, 1871, in Lower Fort Garry and was able to help establish Western Canada. It was the first treaty out of eleven treaties made and is set in the south of Canada. The negotiations started on July 27, 1871. Indigenous, women, men, and children made camp with 100 tents around Fort-Garry getting info from a translater named James McKay. Treaty 1 was signed by Lieutenant-Governor Adams G. Archibald, Commissioner Simpson, Major A.G. Irvine and eight witnesses. Signatories that the Anishinaabe and the Swampy Cree were Red Eagle, Bird Forever, Flying Down Bird, Centre of Bird's Tail, Flying Round, Whip-poor-will, and Yellow Quill. The treaty was made with the understandment that it will last for as long as the sun shines, grass grows, and water flows.

    Who is Apart of Treaty 1?

    There treaty was made between the Anishinaabe and the Crown. The crown was represented by Archibald, Simpson, and Irvine who were there for the current queen at the time. Queen Victoria, who Archibald referred to as "Great Mother." There were also 7 nations who were apart of treaty 1, and represented the Anishinaabe. They were Peguis First Nation, Sagkeeng First Nation, Brokenhead Ojibway Nation, Roseau River Anishinaabe First Nation, Long Plain First Nation, Sandy Bay Ojibway First Nation, and Swan Lake First Nation. All the 7 nations are represented on the Treaty 1 Flag as sun rays.
